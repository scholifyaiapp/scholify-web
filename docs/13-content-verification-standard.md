# Scholify content verification standard

**Owner:** Content & Learning  
**Applies to:** Study chapters, question banks, explanations, flashcards, briefs and written tasks  
**Status:** Mandatory for all new verification work

## 1. What "verified" means

Content is **verified** only when all of the following are true:

1. It maps to a learning outcome in the official syllabus and study guide for the labelled exam year.
2. Every technical claim is checked against an authoritative source.
3. Every calculation is independently recomputed and its units, rounding and assumptions are checked.
4. Every question has exactly one defensible answer under the facts given.
5. The explanation proves the correct answer and explains why plausible distractors fail.
6. The wording is original and does not reproduce ACCA, BPP, Kaplan or another provider's protected material.
7. A second reviewer records their name, date and decision.

Passing a TypeScript test, having the expected number of chapters, or being generated by an AI model does **not** make content verified.

## 2. Evidence hierarchy

Use sources in this order:

1. ACCA syllabus and study guide for the applicable exam year.
2. ACCA examining-team guidance, technical articles and published exam-format guidance.
3. Primary standards and legislation: IFRS Foundation/IASB, IAASB, IFAC/IESBA, and the relevant tax or legal authority.
4. Reputable academic or professional sources where the official materials do not define a concept.
5. BPP and Kaplan only as licensed coverage benchmarks. Never copy, closely paraphrase, or republish their text or questions.

Public ACCA study resources are also copyrighted. They may define scope and support fact-checking, but Scholify must publish original explanations, examples and questions.

## 3. Required lifecycle

`draft -> source_checked -> calculation_checked -> independently_reviewed -> verified`

- **draft:** authored but not relied on as authoritative.
- **source_checked:** syllabus mapping and claim-level evidence recorded.
- **calculation_checked:** all numeric work independently recomputed; use `not_applicable` for non-numeric material.
- **independently_reviewed:** a second qualified person has reviewed the content.
- **verified:** all applicable gates passed with no unresolved finding.

AI may draft or assist with checking, but it cannot be the independent human reviewer.

## 4. Required record per syllabus area

Each record must contain:

- paper code and legacy code;
- syllabus period and effective dates;
- official syllabus area and learning outcomes;
- authoritative source URLs and access dates;
- owned content files and item identifiers;
- verification status for Study, questions, flashcards and written practice;
- calculation-check result;
- reviewer, review date and findings;
- change log when the syllabus or source changes.

## 5. F1 / BT baseline (20 July 2026)

The applicable current source is ACCA's **Business and Technology (BT/FBT) syllabus and study guide, September 2025 to August 2026**. The official exam has six syllabus areas:

- A — The business organisation and its external environment
- B — Organisational structure, culture, governance and sustainability
- C — Business functions, regulation and technology
- D — Leadership and management
- E — Personal effectiveness and communication in business
- F — Professional ethics

Scholify currently exposes four broader areas. Its automated audit reports `4/4`, but this is not equivalent to official six-area blueprint coverage. Until the content is remapped, reviewed and evidenced, F1/BT must be described as **draft, syllabus-oriented content**, not fully verified content.

## 6. Release rule

No UI badge, marketing statement or internal document may call a paper or area "verified" unless its verification record is complete. Partial certification must name the exact scope, for example: "BT Area A Study chapter source-checked for the September 2025–August 2026 syllabus; independent review pending."
